Why Do You Need to Resize Your Images?

Bloggers typically use a lot of images on their websites. If you are purchasing or using free stock imagery on your website, you know that when you download a standard image, it is usually quite large (4000x6000px). That size will bog down your website and slow it to a halt. You need a JPEG resizer before you can use those photos. Resizing.app is the answer. You can pop your photos in and convert them instantly to the perfect size, which won’t slow down your website. Plus, you won’t lose any of the fabulous quality.

Other reasons for resizing your images before uploading, are page load speed, SEO, and smaller images take up less space on the server. If you plan to promote your blog through social media, you will also be limited to size constraints when uploading to the usual platforms (Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, etc.). You may have areas on your website where you need a specific size to fill the canvas, Resizing.app can help there too.

The trick is to use the smallest size possible that still looks crisp and clear. High-resolution images are great for print but not the web. The ideal resolution for web graphics is between 72 and 92dpi.

Where to Get Free Stock Imagery

Purchasing stock imagery to make your blog look professional can get expensive. Thankfully, there are a few free stock image repositories that you can use to grab great-looking photos in just about every category. Some of the best places to get free images are:

  • Unsplash - includes thousands of high-quality photographs for any use online in social media, personal websites, and even business applications.
  • Pexels - a mix of photos and videos that you can use anywhere. It has some limitations as to pictures for every occasion. You can also choose from a partner website (but there may be fees involved).
  • Pixabay - more than 1 million photographs for you to choose from for just about any blog post topic.
  • Canva - millions of free stock photos to use for your blog.
  • Burst - owned by Shopify, this website offers hundreds of stock photos for you to use on your website. Choose between high and low-resolution images.

Although it is not required, they do ask that you give credit to the photographers by mentioning their name in a caption or metadata.
